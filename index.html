Some Stuff About Me

WHO I AM - First & foremost, I am a Dad. I have 2 boys, 1 year old Bryce & 2 year old Bronson. I grew up in a military family, moving frequently to Army bases all over the east coast. After settling down in the Richmond area of Virginia, I consider that my hometown. I have lived in Orlando, FL for about a decade now & will soon be saying "Good-bye!" to the sweltering, unrelenting heat. My wife & I have decided to relocate to Massachusetts, where her family lives. We will be living in the Greater Boston Area, trying to stay within 45 minutes of the city. Our goal is to be there by March 2016. I am Co-Owner, along with my Mother, of a successful neighborhood bar & grill called The Pour House.

I enjoyed the business before I had kids. Late nights, long hours & the bar scene is not the environment I want my boys to grow up in. They say that your children will be more likely to choose the same career path as their parents. I see a future where Developers will continue to be in high demand, so I plan on teaching my boys how to code very early on. I planned on changing careers about a year ago & was introduced to the world of Web Development by my Sister & 2 Cousins (who are all back-end developers) as a career that I'd be perfect for. They were right. I enrolled in a Rails Engineering bootcamp at The Iron Yard in May 2015. The 12 week course was intense, immersive & game changing for me. Let's see....some other important stuff about me: Star Wars over Star Trek, Biggie Over Tupac, I love to laugh & love to make people laugh, I'm not great at writing about myself & most importantly, I am ready & eager to start my new career as a Web Developer.

Intergalactic DJ Name Generator

This was a weekend solo project for the Iron Yard. I had to come up with an idea for & build a functioning Rails application, called a "Mash-up", by combining at least 2 APIs. This Application used the "Star Wars" API & the "Random Word" API. I used Ruby to generate a random Star Wars character's name from the "Star Wars" API, keep only the first name of that character & add "DJ" to the front. I then used the "Random Word" API to append a randomly generated word to the Star Wars character's name. It was fun & I am honestly proud of this silly little app. I built this over a weekend & had never even heard of "mash-ups" until the day prior. I learned a valuable lesson in keeping the scope of the project at a level in which it can be completed on time. I had amazing ideas that I realized were just not possible to complete within the given timeframe. Hey, I still chuckle when I generate names.

  • Client: Intergalactic DJ Name Generator
  • Date: July 2015
  • Category: Web Development
