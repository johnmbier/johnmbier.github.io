Some Stuff About Me

WHO I AM - First & foremost, I am a Dad. I have 2 boys, 1 year old Bryce & 2 year old Bronson. I grew up in a military family, moving frequently to Army bases all over the east coast. After settling down in the Richmond area of Virginia, I consider that my hometown. I have lived in Orlando, FL for about a decade now & will soon be saying "Good-bye!" to the sweltering, unrelenting heat. My wife & I have decided to relocate to Massachusetts, where her family lives. We will be living in the Greater Boston Area, trying to stay within 45 minutes of the city. Our goal is to be there by March 2016. I am Co-Owner, along with my Mother, of a successful neighborhood bar & grill called The Pour House.

I enjoyed the business before I had kids. Late nights, long hours & the bar scene is not the environment I want my boys to grow up in. I planned on changing careers about a year ago & was introduced to the world of Web Development by my Sister & 2 Cousins (who are all back-end developers) as a career that I'd be perfect for. They were right. I enrolled in a Rails Engineering bootcamp at The Iron Yard in May 2015. The 12 week course was intense, immersive & game changing for me. Let's see....some other important stuff about me: Star Wars over Star Trek, Biggie Over Tupac, I love to laugh & love to make people laugh, I'm not great at writing about myself & most importantly, I am ready & eager to start my new career as a Web Developer.

Totin' Tots

Totin Tots was my final project for The Iron Yard. It was a solo project, with an 11 day deadline to complete. This application was developed as a packing list generator for parents traveling with infants and toddlers. The appearance is deceptive in it’s simplicity. There was a lot of Ruby going on behind the scenes in order to allow the user to complete the whole process in a matter of seconds. We built this over a weekend, rolled our own authentication system, built a tagging system, implemented voting, question and answering using Single-Table Inheritance (STI) & even created a catchy name & a cool logo.

Intergalactic DJ Name Generator

This was a weekend project for the Iron Yard, in which I had to think of and build a functioning "Mash-up" web app by combining at least 2 APIs. This Application used the "Star Wars API" & the "Random Word API". I used Ruby to generate a random Star Wars character's name from the "Star Wars" API, keep only the first name of that character & add "DJ" to the front. I then used the "Random Word" API to append a randomly generated word to the Star Wars character's name. It was fun &I am honestly proud of this silly little app. I built this over a weekend & had never even heard of "mash-ups" until the day prior. I learned a valuable lesson in keeping the scope of the project at a level in which it can be completed on time. I had amazing ideas that I realized were just not possible to complete within the given timeframe. Hey, I still chuckle when I generate names.

  • Client: Intergalactic DJ Name Generator
  • Date: July 2015
  • Category: Web Development

Pour House Bar & Grill

The Pour House is a neighborhod sports bar & grill. My most visible contribution upon entering this 8,000 square foot restaurant is the decor, it's vintage sports "art" from the late 1800's to the 1990's. I decided to go the vintage route because there just isn't another sports bar with our nostalgic ambiance, mostly because old school sports memorabilia is so expensive & it would cost a fortune to outfit a restaurant with sports collectibles. I decided to use my experience as a Graphic Designer to create the memorabilia myself. I had a gigantic printer & experience, so I began designing and printing custom artwork. Eventually filling the 100+ pre-existing frames with my own art & even selling some to customers who see it on the walls to this day.

  • Client: Hung Art
  • Date: 2006 - 2009
  • Category: Graphic Design & Print Media
